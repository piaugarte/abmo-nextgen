Family Entrepreneur Spotlight

Terra & Thread.

A small atelier making hand-woven textiles from Philippine heritage fibers — one conversation, one loom, one family at a time.

Founded & led by Sample Name

The origin

It started in my grandmother's garage in 2019, with one inherited backstrap loom and a list of weavers in Abra I'd been writing to for months. I wasn't trying to build a business — I was trying to understand where our family's old blankets had come from, and why no one I knew still made them.

What I found was a whole network of women whose craft was being lost not because no one wanted it, but because no one was connecting them to the people who did. That became the work.

What we do

We commission and sell hand-woven throws, runners, and wearables made by a rotating group of master weavers across Northern Luzon. Every piece carries the weaver's name. We pay upfront, hold small batches, and ship globally from a studio in Quezon City.

A lesson learned

I wish I'd known earlier that slow is not the opposite of serious. For my first two years I apologized constantly — to customers, to buyers, to myself — for how long pieces took. Now I put it in the first line of every listing. The people who stay are the people who want that.

How family can help

Word of mouth is everything in this kind of work. If you know someone designing an interior, furnishing a restaurant, or looking for a meaningful gift — send them our way. And if any cousin wants to learn the business side of a slow-fashion label, we run an apprenticeship every summer. We'd love to have more family in the studio.
